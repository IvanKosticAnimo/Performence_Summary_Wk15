1. Executive Commentary

The week ending 5 July 2026 delivered $25,571 GGR on $683K wagered at a portfolio margin of 3.74%. GGR fell 26.6% versus Week 14's $34,857, however it is important to understand why: wagered volume actually rose 51.1% - meaning significantly more money moved through the tables this week. The lower GGR result comes entirely from margin compression (7.71% down to 3.74%), not from reduced player activity. In plain terms, players played more, but the house kept a smaller share of what was bet - a normal outcome of statistical variance at this volume level. Cumulative GGR across the 15 weeks of reporting now stands at $901,359 on $25.76M wagered at 3.50% portfolio margin.

The defining feature of this week is that all seven days finished in profit - the first week in the entire reporting period without a single loss-making day. Saturday 4 July was the strongest day at $8,173 GGR, driven by City Roulette's best single-day result of the reporting period (+$5,302). Monday 29 June was the softest day at $813 GGR, reflecting low early-week wagering volume ($59K).

Stake.us ($17,675 GGR at 5.09% margin) and Stake.com ($7,741 at 4.98%) together produced effectively all of the week's revenue. HUB B2B (EU) finished modestly positive at $3,115 net - a strong contribution from Gamdom ($4,126, its best week in the reporting period) absorbed losses from Betpanda (-$622) and Cryptorino (-$2,286). Wednesday 1 July produced the week's most pronounced swings, with Port Roulette recording both the best single hour (+$4,407 at 9pm UTC) and the worst (-$3,954 at 4am UTC) within the same calendar day.

2. Alerts and Watch Items

Margin compression despite the highest wagered volume in four weeks. $683K was wagered this week - the most since Week 11 - but the portfolio margin fell 3.97 percentage points from Week 14. No single catastrophic loss event occurred; instead, multiple smaller unfavourable outcomes accumulated across the week. This is variance-driven rather than structural, and the cumulative 15-week margin remains steady at 3.50%.

City Roulette wheel failed to register spin results on 1 July (Incident ID 690, Major). The wheel repeatedly produced "did not register" (DNR) events in a short window - meaning spin results were not captured and rounds could not settle normally, requiring manual intervention. The administrative resolution window ran approximately 4.8 hours. This is a hardware or sensor reliability concern and is the most operationally significant incident of the week. See Section 11 for detail and Section 12 for the recommended follow-up.

Cryptorino and Betpanda both finished negative. Two of HUB B2B (EU)'s largest sub-partners by volume went backwards this week: Cryptorino posted -$2,286 on $34,736 wagered and Betpanda posted -$622 on $42,506 wagered. Between them they processed $77K in wagers at a combined loss of $2,908, significantly suppressing the HUB B2B (EU) total. Worth confirming with the commercial team whether this is normal variance at their wagering scale or an emerging pattern.

14 platform incidents logged during the period. See Section 11 for full incident log analysis. No Critical (P1) incidents occurred this week - an improvement on Week 14. The heaviest single day was Friday 3 July with five incidents, several of which appear connected.

Player Behaviour Insights - Microsoft Clarity (June 2026)

Session recordings, heatmaps and frustration-signal data from Microsoft Clarity across June provide direct evidence of how players actually interact with both games - and help explain some of the engagement numbers above.

1. Who is playing - device and country breakdown. Mobile is the dominant platform for both games. On City Roulette, mobile browsers account for roughly 70% of sessions (Chrome Mobile 62.1% at 2,255 sessions, Mobile Safari 7.4% at 269), with desktop Chrome at 19.9% (721 sessions). On Port Roulette, mobile makes up around 53% (Chrome Mobile 40.9% at 2,324 sessions, Mobile Safari 12.2% at 690), with a larger desktop share (Chrome 27.4% at 1,558 sessions). By country, India leads both games - 60.2% of City Roulette sessions (2,184) and 36.0% of Port Roulette sessions (2,043) - followed by the United States (5.4% City / 13.9% Port), the United Kingdom, and Canada. The practical implication: the majority of our players experience both games on a mobile screen, so mobile experience quality directly drives portfolio revenue.

2. Dead clicks - including on the decommissioned Sky Roulette. Both games show repeated dead and rage clicks on elements that look clickable but do not respond, concentrated in the stats, game history, autoplay and mobile chip selection areas - and in some recorded sessions, players exited immediately after a failed interaction. The most striking example came from a recorded Animo Lobby session (29 June, India, mobile): the player repeatedly tried to click into Sky Roulette - a game that is no longer live - with rapid taps on its "Play" option producing only dead clicks, before a play action finally registered elsewhere several minutes later. Players being able to see and attempt to open a decommissioned game is both a frustration source and a lobby content issue that should be corrected.

3. "Insufficient funds" blocking play. On Port Roulette, recordings captured players hitting repeated "INSUFFICIENT FUNDS" messages that blocked play outright - an avoidable barrier at the exact moment a player is trying to bet. Clearer balance visibility and an easier deposit path would convert these blocked attempts into wagers rather than exits.

Fourteen platform incidents were logged during the 29 June - 5 July reporting window. No Critical (P1) incidents occurred - an improvement on Week 14, which included a Critical power blip on Port Roulette. The most significant event this week was a P2 Major on 1 July (ID 690 - the City Roulette wheel repeatedly failed to register valid spin results, generating a run of "did not register" events that required manual intervention). This is a mechanical reliability concern and has been escalated to the Recommended Actions.

Broadcast / Screen Downtime was again the dominant category at 9 of 14 incidents (64%), consistent with the technical complexity of maintaining live motion-capture and Unreal Engine rendering infrastructure across two simultaneous studio volumes. The two Major broadcast incidents were: no dealer visible on City (29 June, ID 686) and an audio dropout affecting both volumes for approximately 11 minutes during troubleshooting activity (3 July, ID 696).

Friday 3 July was the heaviest single day with five incidents logged, several of which appear connected: a Motive PC freeze (ID 695) led to troubleshooting during which the audio mixer power was interrupted (ID 696). A structured review of that sequence may identify whether the audio loss was preventable. Two incidents affected backend transaction processing (RGS Downtime): a game stuck in "game end" phase on both volumes (29 June, Major, ID 685) resolved via websocket reconnect, and a network delay affecting RGS and wheel synchronisation (3 July, Informational, ID 697). No confirmed revenue impact was identified from either.
